About

We are excited to introduce "Psucculint," a cutting-edge software born from the visionary mind of David Singletary at FSCJ in 2023. This remarkable journey began when David assembled a team of dedicated interns, all driven by the common goal of developing a Gaddis pseudocode linter.

Our mission with Psucculint is clear: to provide a powerful linter that serves both students and professors in the realm of programming logic. For students, Psucculint is a valuable tool for self-assessment, enabling them to check and refine their work as they dive into the intricacies of programming. Professors, on the other hand, can rely on Psucculint to efficiently scan assignments, identify errors, and offer constructive feedback to their students.

At its core, Psucculint aims to simplify the educational journey for budding programmers, making the learning process more accessible and comprehensible. We extend our heartfelt gratitude to everyone involved in this project, from our diligent developers to our meticulous testers. Psucculint is more than just software; it's a bridge that brings students, programmers, and mentors together, fostering a supportive and thriving community.

Thank you for being part of this exciting endeavor! Together, we're shaping a brighter future for programming education.

Psucculint is a product of Florida State College at Jacksonville
